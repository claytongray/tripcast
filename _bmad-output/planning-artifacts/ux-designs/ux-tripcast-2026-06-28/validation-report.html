tripcast — UX Design Validation Report

DESIGN.md + EXPERIENCE.md · ux-tripcast-2026-06-28

The spine pair is a strong, near-ship contract. Every PRD user journey (UJ-1…4) and functional requirement (FR-1…14) resolves to a Key Flow, surface, or component; vocabulary is inherited verbatim from the PRD glossary; both spines obey their canonical shape; and prose is lean and load-bearing. The rubric found 0 critical issues.

The two domain lenses, however, found the spine adequate-trending-thin on its own subject matter: because the email is the product, the execution details that decide inbox placement and rendering — glyph delivery method, dark-mode handling, deliverability/auth headers, subject-line and preheader strategy — are exactly what a downstream consumer needs committed, and several were undefined. Voice was strong but under-written: many load-bearing strings were described, not specified.

Status: findings resolved. All critical and high findings were applied to the spines before finalization (see .memlog.md change entry). DESIGN.md now commits contrast targets, dark-mode pairs + rendering strategy, hosted-PNG glyph delivery, fluid width, capped email radii, an Admin-table visual row, and a CAN-SPAM footer line. EXPERIENCE.md gained an Email Delivery & Inbox Invariants section, every missing string (subjects, preheaders, a rough-weather lexicon, welcome body, validation/geocoding/pay-intent/delete copy, countdown boundaries), and pending/already-expressed/offline states. Findings below are retained for the record and marked resolved.
