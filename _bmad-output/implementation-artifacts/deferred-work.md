## Deferred from: scope/polish backlog (2026-06-30)

- **High-quality date picker for trip dates** — the trip-setup form (Story 1.2, `resources/js/pages/Landing.vue` + `TripSetupRequest`) uses plain date inputs. User wants a polished range picker (start/end) for the polish phase, not now. Requirements to brainstorm when scheduled: range selection with end ≥ start and no past start, strong mobile/touch UX, full accessibility (keyboard nav, screen-reader labels), and library-vs-custom. Promote to a proper story (`bmad-create-story`) when polish work begins; keeping date inputs simple until then means the swap stays a contained frontend change.

- **Dead-simple user feedback capture (lean-launch listening)** — goal: make it as easy as possible for early users to tell us anything, to drive lean post-launch iteration. **Already shipped:** per-digest one-tap thumbs (Story 2.6 — `helped`/`not_helpful`, signed email links → `app/Http/Controllers/EmailAction.php`, one row per `(trip_id, send_date)` in the `feedback` table). **To add (all three):** (1) **Site feedback form/widget** — always-available free-text feedback about the site (floating widget and/or a `/feedback` page); needs its own lightweight model/table (free-text body, optional contact email, source/page, nullable user/trip) — do NOT overload the digest `feedback` table, which is the structured digest-reaction metric. (2) **Post-trip "how was it?" email** — automated, sent after a trip's `return_date` asking how the trip + digests went; new scheduled job/command (cadence cousin of `SendDailyDigests`), login-free signed link to a short form, one-shot per trip (idempotency guard). (3) **Expand digest thumbs to capture optional text** — on the existing feedback confirmation page (`resources/js/pages/email/FeedbackConfirm.vue` / `FeedbackResult.vue`), let users add a free-text comment after tapping; store as a nullable `comment` column on `feedback` or in the new general-feedback table. Keep all three frictionless (no login, minimal fields). Deferred to backlog per user; promote to proper stories (`bmad-create-story`) when scheduled — natural Epic 2/3 lineage (reuses the signed-link + scheduled-command patterns).

- **WeatherAPI Pro+ upgrade — extended horizon via Forecast (14d) + Future (≤300d) API** — currently on a lower tier; the live provider (`app/Services/Weather/WeatherApiProvider.php`) only calls the `forecast.json` endpoint and the horizon is capped by `tripcast.forecast.horizon_days` (default 7, env `TRIPCAST_FORECAST_HORIZON_DAYS`). WeatherAPI **Pro+ ($25/mo or $270/yr)** unlocks **14-day Forecast** + **300-day-ahead Future Weather** + 5M calls + 365-day historical. Vision: for trip days within the 14-day window use `forecast.json` as today; for days beyond 14d (up to 300d) call the **Future API** and label those days **"Projected"** in the digest (distinct visual treatment from real forecast). Could also send an early "trip preview" email when a trip is ≤300 days out. Open questions before committing: (1) **accuracy of Future API** projected days — needs validation, these are climatology-style estimates not true forecasts; (2) how WeatherAPI derives future weather; (3) UX wording/disclaimer for "Projected" so it doesn't read as a real forecast. Deliberately deferred on **cost** ($25/mo) until closer to a UX need — user wants to wait before making the jump but considers it likely necessary for customer UX. Implementation seam is clean: extend the `WeatherProvider` port to select Forecast-vs-Future by day offset, mapping both into the existing `ForecastDay`/`Forecast` shapes with a `projected` flag. Docs: https://www.weatherapi.com/docs/ — Future API: https://www.weatherapi.com/docs/#apis-future. Promote to a proper story (`bmad-create-story`, Epic 2 weather-provider lineage) when scheduled.
